Welcome!

I'm a game developer specializing in programming & designing since 2021.

Based in Ontario, Canada, I studied at York University where I was able to learn from industry professionals and create games with a wide range of people.

As a student, I was able to gain recognition in the field with my project No Escaping the Horde and I continue to strive for improvement with my first online multiplayer game Champ of the Zone. Whenever I have the opportunity to design, I build my games around the moments that players will remember. Focusing on the feelings I want my games to imbue and making sure every decision contributes to that vision.

My Projects

Champ of the Zone

Since 2024 I have been slowly chipping away at this game as a side project. Champ of the Zone is an online 2D fighting game meant to give beginners in the genre access to higher level mindgames without needing to build executional skill. It is my first online game with Unity NGO and is made unique with its approach to characters and combat. Players create their chracter builds by assigning attacks to their binds, letting them find synergies and stacking benefits between attacks. Inspired by games such as YOMI Hustle, Footsies, & Divekick, combat is closer to fencing where rounds are decided in one hit to emphasize the importance of reading your opponent and setting up plays.

C# | NGO & Relay | Aseprite | In Progress | Solo

No Escaping The Horde

Created for a game mechanics course in 2024, No Escaping The Horde is a survivorslike/bullet heaven game. It is built around a unique health mechanic where your health is always being drained and getting damaged makes it drain faster. After completing the project, it was accepted as the only solo-dev game into Toronto Games Week 2025's NextGen Showcase; a curated exhibit of 10 student-made games.

Play C# | Aseprite | 4 Months | Solo

Citizen Jane

In January 2025, student-led club Game Devs @ YorkU ran a large-scale collaborative game project where approximately 60 students worked together to create a game over the course of a semester before it was brought to 2025's Level-Up Showcase. I was brought onto the project to co-lead the game design team, mentoring 7 other students to help to define core mechanics and design levels for the game. This project served as an opportunity to work in a team much larger than those I would otherwise be able to work in while giving me exposure to leading and managing others. Working on the game also built my design skills, giving me an opportunity to practice and lead workshops on level/puzzle design.

Play GDScript | Level | 4 Months | Team of 60+

Misc. Group Project Games

Over the past three years I have been tasked with working in a group for many game projects. These experiences have greatly improved my ability to remain flexible in a group and gain fluency using Git for version control. Many of these projects are short vertical slices made in teams of 4-6 also giving me the chance to build games across multiple genres. These games include dungeon crawlers, first person shooters, survival, party-fighting, and even an experimental 'keepsake' game using a physical stamping component to compliment a choose-your-own-adventure style game.

Play Unity | Godot | C# | Aseprite | 2 Weeks - 4 Months | Team of 4-6

Misc. Solo-Dev Games

As I have been making games for the past five years, I have created a number of small projects that helped grow my proficiency with my tools. Project Lockdown

Made in 8 months and released in 2022, this is my first game project. It was my introduction to Unity and C#, it is a speedrun focused platformer built around a character with a slingshot arm. The game challenges the player to learn levels and improve their execution until they can beat the level before time runs out.

Play Unity | C# | Aseprite | 8 Months | Solo

The Swingshot Trials

My second ever game, this was created in 2 weeks shortly after finishing Project Lockdown to test what I had learned. It is another speedrun focused platformer but I was able to experiment with ideas I couldn't fit into the previous project. You control a ball and use a physics-based rope to swing yourself around the levels as fast as possible. This game also takes a new approach to level design using a simple lock-and-key mechanic and moving away from the tiles used in Project Lockdown in favour of blocking out and drawing over the levels in larger pieces.

Play Unity | C# | Aseprite | 2 Weeks | Solo
