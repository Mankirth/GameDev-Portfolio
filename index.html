Mankirt Hothi

Game Designer & Programmer


Hello! I'm a game developer specializing in programming & designing since 2021. I have always focused on C# and Unity but I have also worked on projects in Godot/GDScript. I always aim to keep player experience as the driving force behind my design decisions, utilizing rapid prototyping and frequent feedback to experiment with ideas.

Based in Ontario, Canada, I earned my degree at York University where I was able to study design & programming while creating games with a wide range of people. Below you can find my projects & my roles in making them.


My Projects

Touché Tempo

Winning #1 for Achievement in Accessibility at Level-Up Showcase 2026, Touché Tempo is a rhythm-fighting game where you use the music to help parry your opponent's attacks in the devil's fencing tournament.

I worked as the main gameplay designer & programmer but also had a hand in background art & sfx.

Play C# | FMOD | Aseprite | 3 Months | Team of 6

Champ of the Zone (In Development)

Champ of the Zone is an online 2D fighting game meant to give beginners in the genre access to higher level mindgames without needing to build executional skill.

It is my first online game with Unity NGO and is made unique with its highly modular loadout-based approach to characters and combat, letting players assign a pool of attacks to their binds directly.

C# | NGO & Relay | Aseprite | In Progress | Solo

No Escaping The Horde

Created for a game mechanics course in 2024, No Escaping The Horde is a survivorslike/bullet heaven game built around a unique health mechanic where your infection is always growing.

After completing the project, it was accepted as the only solo-dev game into Toronto Games Week 2025's NextGen Showcase; a curated exhibit of 10 student-made games.

Play C# | Aseprite | 4 Months | Solo

Citizen Jane

In January 2025, student-led club Game Devs @ YorkU gathered over 60 students to work together in creating a game over the course of a semester before it was brought to 2025's Level-Up Showcase. Citizen Jane is the resulting game, a comic-inspired narrative mystery game where you navigate through panels and solve puzzles.

I worked on this project as a co-lead for the game design team, mentoring 7 other students to help define core mechanics and design levels for the game while leading workshops on level/puzzle design.

Play GDScript | 4 Months | Team of 60+

Low Health Saviour (Peak Mod)

Low Health Saviour is a recreation of the 'Divine Intervention' Minecraft mod for Peak that builds on the original by adding comprehensive customization for the player. This project was the first the I had the opportunity to learn from and build onto an industry-level codebase, the practices and architecture I learned from this project is still applied to all of my current work.

This project was also able to gain a lot of popularity in the community, gaining over 20,000 downloads and being featured on popular livestreams.

Play Unity | C# | 2 Weeks | Solo

Project Lockdown

Made in 8 months and released in 2022, Project Lockdown is a speedrun focused platformer built around a character with a slingshot arm. The game challenges the player to learn levels and improve their execution until they can beat the level before time runs out.

This is my first game project. It was my introduction to Unity, C#, and my first time practicing game design.

Play Unity | C# | Aseprite | 8 Months | Solo

The Swingshot Trials

The Swingshot Trials was created in 2 weeks shortly after finishing Project Lockdown to test what I had learned. You control a ball and use a physics-based rope to swing yourself around the levels as fast as possible.

As my second ever game, I used this project as an opportunity to imrpove with what I learned, taking a more flexible approach to game & level design while gaining fluency in being able to program more complex mechanics.

Play Unity | C# | Aseprite | 2 Weeks | Solo

Additional Group Project Games

Over the past three years I have worked in many small groups for game projects. These experiences have greatly improved my ability to remain flexible in a group and gain fluency using Git for version control. Many of these projects are short vertical slices made in teams of 4-6, also giving me the chance to build games across multiple genres.

I worked as a main programmer and designer on all of these projects, giving me opportunities to experiment with new ideas and technologies while building my skills.

Play Unity | Godot | C# | Aseprite | 2 Weeks - 4 Months | Team of 4-6

Additional Solo-Dev Games

As I have been making games for the past five years, I have created a number of small projects that helped grow my proficiency with my tools. The dev time of these games range from less than a week to a few months but they were all made in an effort to quickly try something new.

Some of these games include my first time making games with Godot, SQL Databases, and GBStudio. This collection also further explores some genres and concepts that I haden't designed for before such as endless runners, turn-based combat systems, cooking games, and idle games.

Play Unity | Godot | C# | GDScript | Aseprite | 3 Days - 4 Months | Solo
